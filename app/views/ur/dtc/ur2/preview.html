Victim Personal Statement

Ahead of any sentencing hearings, we will provide the court with your ‘Victim Personal Statement’ (VPS) if you have provided one. A VPS is an opportunity to explain in your own words how the crime has impacted you; the judge or magistrates will take this into consideration during sentencing. The police will ask you if you’d like to provide a VPS during the investigation. You can update or make a further VPS at any stage of the case. You can get in touch with your police contact who will arrange for you to do this.

The Online Victims Guide

The online Victims Guide is an information portal designed by the Crown Prosecution Service (CPS) to provide you with more information on what you can expect as the case progresses through the criminal justice system.

It highlights what support is available to help you through the end-to-end process, including information and available support should you be asked to give evidence. You can access the online guide here: https://www.cps.gov.uk/information-and-support-victims-witnesses

Further Support

  • There are many support services available to you. This website may help you find support that is right for you: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/victim-and-witness-services#support-for-victims-and-witnesses-of-crime
  • If you would like to talk to someone not connected to the prosecution, you could contact Victim Support who offer free and confidential advice. Their contact number is 0808 1689111. You can also log onto their website: https://www.victimsupport.org.uk/
  • For more general, urgent support you can contact your local NHS Foundation Trust or the Samaritans:
  • To find your local NHS service please visit https://www.nhs.uk/service-search/mental-health/find-an-urgent-mental-health-helpline and answer two questions to find the right helpline.
  • The Samaritans provide a 24/7 listening service on 116 123 where you can talk through your concerns, worries and troubles. You can also email them on jo@samaritans.org or visit the website for a wide range of support services including financial worries, relationship and family problems and job-related stress https://www.samaritans.org/.

Further Information

  • You have rights as a victim. These rights are explained in the Code of Practice for Victims of Crime, which you can find here: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/the-code-of-practice-for-victims-of-crime
  • If you want to make a complaint or give feedback, you can do so here: https://contact.cps.gov.uk/
  • If you are a victim of a crime, you may be able to get compensation. This link may help you to find out more:
  • Claiming Compensation (please note time limits may apply): https://www.victimandwitnessinformation.org.uk/claim-compensation/
